About the project

Retention is cheaper than acquisition

Churn prediction turns a lagging indicator — a customer who already left — into a leading one: a risk score you can act on while the relationship is still saveable.

The business problem

Subscription and telecom businesses lose 15–25% of customers annually. Most of that revenue is invisible until the cancellation request lands — by which point it's too late to intervene.

The project goal

Build a classification system that scores every active customer's likelihood of churning, using account, billing, and service signals already sitting in the CRM — no new data collection required.

Why it matters

A 5-point improvement in retention can lift lifetime profit by 25–95% (Bain & Co.). Even a modestly accurate model pays for itself the first month it's used to trigger a save offer.

Flags at-risk customers weeks before they cancel
Prioritises retention spend on customers worth saving
Surfaces which contract and billing terms drive churn
Plugs into any CRM through a lightweight REST endpoint

Data preprocessing

From raw records to model-ready vectors

01

Missing values

Blank TotalCharges (new accounts) imputed from tenure × monthly rate.

02

Label encoding

Binary fields (gender, partner, dependents, billing) mapped to 0/1.

03

One-hot encoding

Contract, internet service, and payment method expanded into indicator columns.

04

Feature scaling

Numeric features standardised with StandardScaler before training.

05

Train/test split

80/20 stratified split to preserve the churn/stay class ratio.
